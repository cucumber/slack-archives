*Thread Reply:* In my opinion, I hate seeing multiple “when’s” within a scenario. The When should have your verb. As in, this step should really be what you’re testing, your “then” step can be the place to assert that your “when” genuinely happened, and to behavior it should have.

When I see many when steps within a single scenario I begin to ask “what are you really testing”? Often times if you have more than one when, you can simply make them into two (if you had two when steps) distinct and more clear scenarios.
