thomas
2018-04-03 07:18:14

Thanks for your question. Most of the folks who attend this course have at least a few years experience in software development, in some cases, decades. We rarely get feedback that this course isn't challenging.

While the course covers the fundamentals of BDD, it is suitable for even the most accomplished and experienced developers, testers and BAs to think differently about how they develop and deliver software.

The more experience you have of software development, the easier it will be to understand what we are practising and really benefit from it. Inexperienced developers might have a hard time to understand the problem we are trying to solve. That is, bridging the communication gap between development and business. They haven't felt the pain of misunderstanding enough too see it as a big problem. It took me a long time to appreciate that pain myself.

With a few years experience, I suspect you'll find this course more valuable than if you were a beginner.
